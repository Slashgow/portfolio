Paper Tower

About

Paper Tower is a transparent desktop-overlay tower defense and idler game built in Unity for Windows. It runs quietly over your other windows while you work, combining a modular tower/fusion system, wave-based enemy spawning, and a built-in Pomodoro timer that ties productivity to in-game progress.

Project Info

  • Role: Game Developer
  • Team Size: 1
  • Status: In development
  • Engine: Unity (URP, C#)

Introduction

Paper Tower started as an experiment in combining two things I like: idle/tower-defense systems and a desktop tool I'd actually want running while I work. The result is a transparent overlay window — built on Win32/DWM APIs — that hosts a full tower defense layer on top of whatever else is on your screen, paired with a Pomodoro timer that feeds directly into the game's progression.

On the systems side, the core loop centers on a modular tower/fusion system: modules can be combined up to three at a time, with a shiny variant tier unlocked through level-3 fusion, each with its own bespoke behavior. A centralized TickManager replaces per-object Update() calls to keep performance predictable as the number of active modules grows, and an event-driven architecture (static C# events) keeps UI, save data, and gameplay systems decoupled.

Wave design is backed by a dedicated stats and recap system — per-module and per-damage-type tracking, serialized wave history, and chart-based breakdowns — so balancing decisions are based on real data rather than guesswork. Boss encounters (starting with BossRenard's fire zones, wind deflection, and meteor attacks) are built as their own ability sets on top of the same targeting and projectile systems used by regular towers.
